What if onboarding weren’t a slideshow to click through, but an interactive, role-aware experience a new hire actually learns from?

The gap

Generic onboarding makes everyone read the same passive slides. New hires need to practice the real workflow, customized to their role, not just watch it, or it doesn’t transfer to day one.

Showcase 01 , Instructional Design

A self-paced onboarding module, built in Articulate Rise

Designer’s note

A 15-lesson, self-paced module that onboards new hires to a cross-functional project-management workflow, in three tiers (Basic, Medium, Advanced). Each lesson opens with measurable objectives and uses guided interactions instead of passive reading. Its most advanced lesson teaches dependency literacy, reading what your task blocks and is blocked by, so a team of eight functions stays in sync.

What if a course team spanning eight functions never had to ask “whose turn is it?”

The gap

A course needs eight functional teams (ID, video, multimedia, build, QA…) handing work back and forth. Without shared structure, no one knows whose turn it is, what’s blocked, or where a course actually stands.

Showcase 03 · Systems & Operations

A course-development task-management system

Designer’s note

Designed from interviews across every functional team: a standardized task-management workflow with clear ownership, milestone gates, and dependency logic, implemented in the team’s project tool with automation that rolls completed tasks up into a live status dashboard.

Adopted daily across all eight functional teams, it turned a tangle of hand-offs into one legible pipeline.

The AI Lab

Teaching complex AI concepts through interaction. The AI Lab is a set of explorable explainers I designed and built, interactive demos, not slides, that let a learner understand how modern AI behaves by changing inputs and watching what happens. Three are recreated here in brief.

The gap

Non-technical teams are asked to adopt AI, but slide decks can’t convey a dynamic system. People need to feel how it behaves to trust and use it well.
